üß† What is Emotional Volatility Arbitrage?

Prediction markets like Polymarket are dominated by retail traders who make decisions based on emotion, recency bias, and partisan beliefs rather than statistical reasoning. This creates predictable patterns of overreaction and subsequent correction that can be systematically exploited.

The Core Mechanism

When news breaks or sentiment shifts rapidly, traders overreact:

  • Positive news ‚Üí Rush to buy ‚Üí Prices spike above fair value
  • Negative news ‚Üí Panic selling ‚Üí Prices crash below fair value
  • Time passes ‚Üí Emotions fade ‚Üí Prices revert to fundamentals
Example: A candidate says something controversial in a debate. Their election odds drop from 65% to 48% in minutes. But historical data shows debate performance has minimal correlation with election outcomes. Within 48 hours, the price reverts to 62%. That 14-point swing is pure emotion.

üéØ Why This Works

1. Behavioral Finance Foundations

Decades of research show retail traders consistently:

  • Overreact to recent information (recency bias)
  • Overvalue extreme probabilities (longshot bias)
  • Hold partisan beliefs that distort their probability assessments
  • Chase momentum and create self-reinforcing spirals

2. Market Structure Advantages

  • No professional market makers to arbitrage away inefficiencies
  • Retail-dominated participant base (86.4% lose money on Polymarket)
  • Low capital requirements prevent large institutions from entering
  • Political markets especially irrational due to partisan emotions

3. Mean Reversion at Specific Horizons

Berg & Rietz (2018) found that prediction market mispricing mean-reverts most strongly at 7-21 day horizons. This is our sweet spot:

  • Too short (<3 days): Noise dominates, hard to profit
  • Just right (7-21 days): Emotional overreactions correct
  • Too long (>30 days): New information dominates, reversion less reliable
